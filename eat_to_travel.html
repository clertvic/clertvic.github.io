Eat to Travel

World tour in the kitchen

Eat to Travel is a conceptual iPad magazine designed to create a new travel experience while cooking and eating. Each issue features dessert recipes from different countries around the world. Every recipe is associated with a compelling story and stunning photos related to a destination where the recipe is originally from. This added immersive experience allows users to experience and savor their favorite destinations or explore places that they have never been before.

Exploratory Research

To find out what products currently exist in this space, we conducted a competitive analysis by looking at several culinary products from both offline and online services. After evaluating each product, we found that there are three main product types in the space. The first product type or recipe databases aim at providing users with the most comprehensive repository of recipes. The second product type or blogs focus on the storytelling, which takes users to a specific location by using personal stories. The last product type or magazines tend to bring an immersive experience to users through beautiful pictures and stories.

Since the recipe databases are pervasive and easy to find, our final concept was focused around the storytelling and immersive experience. We also found that food and travel are closely related. By convention, we get to eat a lot of tasty and unique foods while traveling. However, we thought that it would be interesting to flip and create a new travel experience through interesting stories and beautiful pictures while cooking and eating.

Design Process

To help us design a digital magazine that reflects the needs of our users, we developed three personas to identify users with different motivations. The first persona represented users who want to explore places they have never visited. The second persona reflected users who want to revisit their favorite places. The last persona portrayed users who are far away from home and want to experience the taste of their native land. All of them, of course, like making desserts or just start learning how to make ones.

After creating personas, we started developing layout options with emphasis on design language and navigation to ensure legibility, consistency, and ease of use throughout the magazine. The design language consisted of typography and color palette. When it comes to typography, we tried out different typefaces and type sizes to establish hierarchy. For the color selection process, we created a mood board consisting of text and images from various paper magazines.
