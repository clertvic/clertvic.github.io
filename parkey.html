Parkey

A key to quick and easy parking

Parkey is a conceptual iPhone app designed to help you quickly find and easily pay for your parking. By paying through your phone, Parkey helps eliminate problems associated with parking such as having no quarters, finding the right meter for your parking, or understanding street signs. Parkey allows you to extend parking time when your meter is running low. It also remembers where you parked and shows you turn-by-turn walking direction from your current location to your car. If you find any illegally parked vehicles irritating, you can report them to authority with Parkey!

User Research

We conducted user research to understand the problem space and identify opportunities to make parking simpler by using mobile phone. The user research included surveys and interviews with drivers and garage attendants. Informed by our research findings, we identified our target demographic and developed personas that represent different types of potential users. We also built scenarios to communicate the context of use for these personas.

Design Process

Moving forward to screen-based design, we created a set of wireframes to investigate the navigation flow and functionality of the app. We conducted user-testing sessions with our classmates to get feedback on our wireframes. During the sessions, we asked them to think aloud while they performed specific tasks.

After validating our wireframes, we started thinking about design language such as fonts and colors to ensure consistency of visual designs throughout the app. We also thought about the logo and app icon and iteratively improved the design based on peer feedback.

Final Deliverable

Our final deliverable was a set of high-fidelity visual designs for selected screens highlighting Parkey's key features. To demonstrate how our target users would use Parkey, we created animated transitions of our high-fidelity screens in Keynote and presented them through a scenario in our final pitch presentation.
